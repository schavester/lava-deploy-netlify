Join The Echo Park Film Center<\/a> and their green-energy mobile cinema for a screening, outside the Watts Towers, of the BBC documentary Reyner Banham Loves Los Angeles<\/em> (1972).<\/p>\n

This ground-breaking film is the English art historian\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s love letter to Los Angeles. From the Sunset Strip to Venice Beach, the Gamble House to Tiny Naylor\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s, from fanciful proto-GPS<\/span> systems named \u00e2\u20ac\u0153Baede-kar\u00e2\u20ac\u009d to Vernon to fiberglass fabrication shops of South La Cienega, this snapshot of 1972 Los Angeles remains piercing and highly relevant today.<\/p>\n

LAVA<\/span> co-founder Richard Schave, a student of Banham\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s at UC Santa Cruz in the mid-1980s and host of Esotouric\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s series of Banham-inspired California Culture<\/a> bus tours of Los Angeles, will introduce the film.<\/p>\n

The screening will occur at dusk and outdoors just outside the Watts Towers complex. Please dress accordingly.<\/p>\n
