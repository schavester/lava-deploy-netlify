Marguerite Darlington is a Los Angeles-based writer and editor. She graduated from Northwestern University\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s Medill School of Journalism. <\/span>Her career began in academic publishing as a copy editor for the journal \u00e2\u20ac\u0153Literature and Medicine,\u00e2\u20ac\u009d and then she pursued integrated marketing at Integrated Marketing Solutions (IMS) in Chicago, Illinois.<\/span><\/p>\n

During the dot com boom, Marguerite wrote and developed web sites for feature films and entertainment properties for \u00e2\u20ac\u0153The Sixth Sense,\u00e2\u20ac\u009d \u00e2\u20ac\u0153The X-Files,\u00e2\u20ac\u009d \u00e2\u20ac\u0153Muppet World\u00e2\u20ac\u009d and more at Dream Theater in Chatsworth, California. Then Marguerite joined the staff of the Marketing Services department at<\/span> <\/span>The New York Times<\/em>. She worked as a copywriter, creating sell sheets and advertising materials for special advertising sections.<\/span><\/p>\n

For the past ten years, Marguerite has worked as a freelance writer and editor, taking on projects that stimulate her curious mind and growth as an individual. Currently, Marguerite serves as managing editor for<\/span> <\/span>fashionablymarketing.me<\/a>. She is active on social media, and invites you to follow her on <\/span>Twitter<\/a>, <\/span>Facebook<\/a>, <\/span>LinkedIn<\/a>, <\/span>Tumblr<\/a>, GoodReads, <\/span>Pinterest<\/a> and <\/span>YouTube<\/a>.<\/span><\/p>\n

In her spare time, she writes mystery novels and short stories. She lives in Los Angeles, California, with her husband Joshua, their notorious pug, Monty, and Rajah the jungle cat.<\/p>\n
