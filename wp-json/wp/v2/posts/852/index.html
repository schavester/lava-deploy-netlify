The Bob Baker Marionette Theater continues its 52nd season with \u00e2\u20ac\u0153Bob Baker\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s Halloween<\/span> Hoop-Dee-Do\u00e2\u20ac\u009d playing October 6-November 10, 2012. 
<\/span><\/span><\/span>
<\/span><\/span>One of the theater\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s perennial favorites, \u00e2\u20ac\u0153Bob Baker\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s Halloween<\/span> Hoop-De-Do\u00e2\u20ac\u009d first played the theater in 1963.  Featuring a fantastical cast of over 100 Halloween<\/span>themed puppets, from the Purple People Eater and the Invisible Man to a gaggle of Roaring 20’s skeletons dancing the night away in Hernando’s Hideaway, \u00e2\u20ac\u0153Bob Baker\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s Halloween<\/span> Hoop-De-Do\u00e2\u20ac\u009d will whisk you away to \u00e2\u20ac\u0153the place where imagination dwells\u00e2\u20ac\u009d.  
<\/span><\/span><\/span>
<\/span><\/span>These celebrated marionettes are presented in Bob Baker\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s now famous \u00e2\u20ac\u0153In the Round\u00e2\u20ac\u009d cabaret style, with the puppeteers not only exposed to the audience, but serving as an integral part of the proceedings . . . and also making the audience part of the show. 
 
\u00e2\u20ac\u0153Bob Baker\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s Halloween<\/span> Hoop-De-Do\u00e2\u20ac\u009d is an hour-long revue. After the performance, guests are invited to visit with the Puppeteers and have refreshments in our Party Room.<\/span><\/span><\/span><\/p>\n
