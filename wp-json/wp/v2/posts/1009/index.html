Discover the story of Hope Foye, a progressive young singer whose voice impressed legends such as Paul Robeson and W.E.B. Dubois. However, a subpeona to appear before the McCarren Committee in 1949 changed her life forever.  “Red Hope: The Blacklisting of Hope Foye” will be screened at the Mayme Clayton Library and Museum in Culver City. The film’s subject, Hope Foye, will be at the screening to discuss her life and career. <\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n
