Los Angeles Historic Theatre Foundation and Hollywood Heritage present<\/p>\n

ALL ABOUT GRAUMAN\u00e2\u20ac\u2122S CHINESE THEATRE<\/p>\n

A behind-the-scenes tour of the world\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s most famous movie palace to celebrate its 85th Anniversary<\/p>\n

Saturday, May 26 and Sunday, May 27, 8:30 am \u00e2\u20ac\u201c Tours will be completed by Noon<\/p>\n

Advance Sale Members-Only: Admission: $10<\/p>\n

Hollywood Heritage and Los Angeles Historic Theatre Foundation members only (a limited number of tickets are available to members at this price)<\/p>\n

Member tickets on sale April 19 \u00e2\u20ac\u201c April 30 thru PayPal at www.lahtf.org<\/a><\/p>\n

General Admission: $20 \u00e2\u20ac\u201c on sale May 1 thru PayPal at www.lahtf.org <\/a><\/p>\n

All About Grauman\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s Chinese Theatre is graciously hosted by Chinese Theatres, LLC.  This site<\/a> is a great way to get to know the Chinese Theatres.<\/p>\n

Los Angeles Historic Theatre Foundation<\/a> & Hollywood Heritage<\/a> in association with the American Cinematheque and Art Deco Society of L.A., invite you to a comprehensive behind-the-scenes documentary tour of Sid Grauman\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s masterpiece, the Chinese Theatre, to celebrate its 85th Anniversary. All About Grauman\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s Chinese Theatre will take you on an insider\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s tour: backstage, under-stage, to Grauman\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s office and private boxes, down the stars\u00e2\u20ac\u2122 stairway and upstairs to the new Chinese Ballroom bar and restaurant, through the renovated Chinese 6 theatres and to Grauman\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s Gallery, featuring photos of the theatre\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s storied history. Grauman expert, Mark Simon, will share the Sid Grauman story, from the Yukon to L.A. Theatre historian Ed Kelsey will present a PowerPoint history of the Chinese: the planning, construction and operation of the theatre, its elaborate prologues, the Forecourt and its iconic imprint ceremonies, and explain the theatre\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s evolution since its grand opening on May 18, 1927. Why has Grauman\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s Chinese Theatre survived as the only purpose-built movie palace in the world showing first-run films since its opening day? And ChineseTheatres management will discuss the operation of the theatre and plans for its future.<\/p>\n

The Los Angeles Historic Theatre Foundation celebrates its 25th Anniversary protecting, preserving, restoring and sustaining the operation of L.A.\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s historic theatres. Hollywood Heritage marks more than 40 years preserving Hollywood\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s history and protecting its built environment. Together, these two vital preservation organizations salute Grauman\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s Chinese Theatre, a landmark visited by more than 8 million people each year, by offering this extraordinary 85th Anniversary tour.<\/p>\n
