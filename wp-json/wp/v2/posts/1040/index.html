Raymond Thornton Chandler (July 23, 1888 \u00e2\u20ac\u201c March 26, 1959)<\/em><\/p>\n

Tonight, LAVA<\/span> celebrates the great Los Angeles novelist Raymond Chandler<\/a> on the 125th anniversary of his birth, in the historic spaces where he got a priceless education in civic corruption, wealth and vice.<\/p>\n

Please join us at the Los Angeles Athletic Club at the corner of 7th and Olive Street, Chandler\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s old stomping grounds, for a celebration of his life and work. We\u00e2\u20ac\u2122ll gather in the club\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s newly redecorated third floor bar, Invention<\/em>, where the young oil executive played bridge and avoided returning to the offices of the Dabney Oil Syndicate, which can be viewed through the bar\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s tall windows. Later, we\u00e2\u20ac\u2122ll move down the block to the Oviatt Building, the seat of power for\u00c2\u00a0<\/span>Chandler\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s greatest villain, Derace Kingsley (not-so-loosely based on James Oviatt), in the novel The Lady in the Lake<\/a><\/em>, before returning to the club for a last toast to the departed.<\/span><\/p>\n

The evening will include readings and musings on Chandler\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s legacy and his impact on how we see Los Angeles\u00e2\u20ac\u201dpast, present and future. There will be a no-host bar, and opportunities to order bar food.<\/p>\n

Space is limited and reservations are required for this free event, and can be made on this page after 10am on July 8.
