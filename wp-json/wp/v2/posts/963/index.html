Join us as we explore Sid Grauman\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s first Los Angeles movie palace. The M$ set a new standard for the national movie palace building boom that swept the U.S. during the 1920\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s. Designed by architect William Lee Woollett, the M$ is unique \u00e2\u20ac\u201c a daring mixture of styles,art and colors. Described as a Spanish cathedral set in a Greek temple, the interior was a riot of rich colors, original art works and lush patron amenities. The M$ is currently closed to the public. Don\u00e2\u20ac\u2122t miss this chance to see this amazing work of art.<\/p>\n

 <\/p>\n

More than any other Broadway theatre, the many glories of the M$ are faded or completely hidden from view. Ed Kelsey will highlight the history, illustrate the many interior alterations and celebrate what remains. We\u00e2\u20ac\u2122ll take you behind the scenes – from the basement to the balcony – and share the hidden treasures of L.A.\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s most unusual movie palace.<\/p>\n
