ABOUT THIS SALON<\/strong>:<\/span><\/p>\n

Charles Bukowski is internationally recognized as an iconic Los Angeles poet and novelist, a voice of the underclass and of the gentleness hiding beneath a prickly exterior.<\/p>\n

You are cordially invited to celebrate all the sides of Charles Bukowski at a LAVA<\/span> Literary Salon, on the evening of Saturday, October 26.<\/p>\n

Your ticket entitles you to entry to the event, which includes a buffet dinner featuring mid-century gourmet fare and the presentations. There will be a cash bar featuring drink specials as well as a complementary glass from Charles Smith Wines. <\/span><\/p>\n

    \n
  • Dan Fante<\/a>: the author most recently of Point Doom<\/a><\/em> will talk about Bukowski\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s relationship with his father, the great L.A. novelist John Fante.<\/li>\n
  • Suzanne Lummis<\/a>: the noted Los Angeles poet will share her memories of Bukowski, and discuss his seminal role in the development of the Los Angeles poetry scene.<\/li>\n
  • Melanie Villines<\/a>: the editor of Bukowski: An Anthology of Poetry & Prose About Charles Bukowski<\/em> (Silver Birch Press, 2013), will discuss this project and Bukowski\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s continued literary influence, two decades after his death.<\/li>\n
  • Thomas Hyry<\/a>: the Director of UCLA Library Special Collections will share material from the John Fante Papers<\/a> that illuminate Bukowski’s relationship with the older writer he called “my God.”<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    ABOUT THE SERIES<\/strong><\/p>\n

    The LAVA<\/span> Literary Salon is a place for lovers of great Los Angeles writers to come together in historic spaces for good company, fine food and fascinating discussions by experts in the field. Join us in the historic Los Angeles Athletic Club, where Raymond Chandler, then a young oil executive, played bridge and eavesdropped on the powerful men who would shape the city and his detective fiction, as we honor the writers who gave this young city its abiding personality. <\/p>\n

    Past Salons have celebrated the life and work of <\/span>John Fante<\/a>, <\/span>Raymond Chandler<\/a>, <\/span>Jim Tully<\/a>, <\/span>F. Scott Fitzgerald and Dorothy Parker<\/a>, Dashiell Hammett<\/a> <\/span>and the booksellers Jake Zeitlin, Stanley Rose, Louis Epstein (Pickwick Books) and Ernest Dawson (Dawson\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s Bookshop).<\/span><\/p>\n
