2012 EL SEGUNDO AUTHOR FAIR<\/p>\n

LAVA member Manny Pacheco will moderate a Pop Culture ’60s and ’70s<\/strong> panel discussion. Featured on this distinguished dais:<\/p>\n

DYAN CANNON<\/strong><\/span> – An award-winning actress (Golden Globe<\/strong>) who starred in popular motion pictures, including Bob & Carol & Ted & Alice<\/strong><\/em>, The Last of Sheila<\/strong><\/em>, and Heaven Can Wait<\/strong><\/em>. Her television credits include Bat Masterson<\/strong><\/em>, Gunsmoke<\/strong><\/em>, 77 Sunset Strip<\/strong><\/em>, The Rockford Files<\/strong><\/em>, and Ally MacBeal<\/strong><\/em>. Next to Jack Nicholson, Dyan is the most visible Los Angeles Lakers<\/strong> fan in Southern California. For three years, she was married to Cary Grant. She has written a book telling her personal story about their 1960s marriage.<\/p>\n

KATHRYN LEIGH SCOTT<\/strong><\/span> \u00e2\u20ac\u201c For male television viewers of a certain age, she was a heartthrob during her run as Maggie Evans<\/em> and Josette Collins<\/em> in the daytime television program Dark Shadows<\/strong><\/em>. She was the love interest of Barnabas Collins<\/em>, played by Jonathan Frid. The popularity of the soap opera spawned the film The House of Dark Shadows<\/strong><\/em> in 1970. She\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s expected to have a cameo role in the 2012 version of Dark Shadows<\/strong><\/em>, produced by Tim Burton and Johnny Depp. Kathryn had a reliable television career, appearing in Cagney<\/strong><\/em> <\/strong> and Lacey<\/strong>, Magnum P.I.<\/strong><\/em>, Quincy<\/strong><\/em>, Matlock<\/strong><\/em>, The A-Team<\/strong><\/em>, The Incredible Hulk<\/strong><\/em>, and Star Trek: The Next Generation<\/strong><\/em>. She also had a role in The Great Gatsby<\/strong><\/em>.<\/p>\n

BILL HUDSON<\/strong><\/span> <\/span>– One third of the Hudson Brothers singing group. He also had very familiar spouses, including Goldie Hawn and Cindy Williams. He\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s the father of actress Kate Hudson. The Hudson Brothers hosted a variety show that was a Summer replacement on CBS <\/strong>in 1974. A noted producer during the height of their output was Bernie Taupin. Bill\u00e2\u20ac\u2122s new book chronicles his singing career, and his own family story.<\/p>\n

HENRY DILTZ<\/strong><\/span> – The official photographer at Woodstock <\/strong>and also participated at the Monterey Festival<\/strong>, Diltz was the prolific rock \u00e2\u20ac\u02dcn roll visual historian. Among the performers he has captured include Michael Jackson, The Doors, Paul and Linda McCartney, The Kingston Trio, Linda Ronstadt, Jackson Browne, The Mamas and Papas, The Monkees, The Byrds, Richard Pryor, and Frank Zappa. His photos were the canvas for iconic album covers of James Taylor, Crosby Stills & Nash, Jimi Hendrix, Joni Mitchell, The Eagles, and Garth Brooks. Over a hundred musical acts have used the conceptual vision of Henry Diltz.<\/p>\n

MANNY PACHECO is the award-winning author of the FORGOTTEN HOLLYWOOD Book Series; and a Southern California television and radio personality.<\/p>\n
