BRIAN KAISER : PERSONAL BIOGRAPHY

  

MR. KAISER IS A NATIVE OF FREMONT, OHIO, WHERE HE WAS RAISED ON A SMALL FARM.  HE ATTENDED BOWLING GREEN STATE UNIVERSITY, WHERE HE ACQUIRED A CUM LAUDE B.A., AND SUMMA CUM LAUDE M.A. IN SPEECH, THEATRE, HISTORY, AND PHILOSOPHY.

 

    HE IS A PROFESSIONAL ACTOR WHO CAME TO CALIFORNIA IN 1981, AND HAS APPEARED IN NUMEROUS COMMERCIALS AND TV SITCOMS. BRIAN ALSO MANAGED A EUROPEAN ANTIQUE IMPORT BUSINESS IN BURBANK FOR 20 YEARS. MR. KAISER ALSO WORKS IN ARCHITECTURAL SALVAGE, WITH AN EMPHASIS ON ANTIQUE TILE. BRIAN HAS ALWAYS ENJOYED ANTIQUES, HISTORY, ARCHITECTURE, AND “A GOOD STORY”.

 

    SINCE 1987, MR. KAISER HAS OWNED THE HOME OF RUFUS KEELER, FOUNDER AND PLANT MANAGER OF THE CALCO AND MALIBU POTTERIES. HE HAS EXTENSIVELY RESEARCHED MR. KEELER’S LIFE, INCLUDING MANY INTERVIEWS WITH KEELER’S FAMILY, AND SEVERAL YEARS OF EXCAVATION AT THE ORIGINAL CALCO POTTERY SITE.

 

    OWNING MR. KEELER’S HOME, HAS MADE BRIAN FEEL LIKE A SPECIAL EXTENSION OF THE KEELER FAMILY, AND HOPES TO SOME DAY WRITE A BOOK ON MR. KEELER, HIS HOME, AND HIS AMAZING WORK. THE MALIBU LAGOON MUSEUM HAS ALREADY PRODUCED TWO BEAUTIFUL BOOKS ON THE MALIBU POTTERIES; BUT CALCO, MR. KEELER’S HOME, AND MUCH OF HIS OTHER WORK HAS BEEN LARGELY NEGLECTED, AND NOT PUBLISHED.

 

RIZZOLI PUBLISHING HAS RECENTLY TAKEN A SERIES OF PROFESSIONAL PICTURES OF MR. KEELER’S HOME, AND THE HOUSE WILL BE FEATURED IN CASA PACIFICA, THEIR NEXT BOOK ON SPANISH COLONIAL ARCHITECTURE ON THE WEST COAST. 

 

IN THE MIDST OF TRACKING DOWN AND IDENTIFYING MANY ANTIQUE TILE INSTALLATIONS, BRIAN HAD TO LEARN HOW TO EXTRACT AND CLEAN VINTAGE TILE, SO IT COULD BE SAVED FROM DEMOLITION OR REMODELING. IMMENSE QUANTITIES OF OUR CERAMIC HERITAGE ARE BEING DESTROYED EVERY DAY.

 

MR. KAISER’S GREATEST FEAT OF URBAN ARCHAEOLOGY OCCURRED AT THE HOLLYWOOD ROOSEVELT HOTEL; WHERE HE RESEARCHED, EXPLORED, DISCOVERED, UNCOVERED, AND RESTORED THE ORIGINAL MALIBU TILE WALL FOUNTAIN IN THE BLOSSOM BALLROOM. THE FOUNTAIN WAS THERE IN 1929, WHEN THE FIRST ACADEMY AWARDS WERE PRESENTED, BUT HAD BEEN PLASTERED INTO THE WALL FOR ABOUT 75 YEARS! THIS FOUNTAIN IS THE LAST REMAINING ORIGINAL DECORATIVE ELEMENT OF THE BLOSSOM BALLROOM. EVERYTHING ELSE HAS BEEN DESTROYED OVER THE YEARS THROUGH INCREDIBLY THOUGHTLESS AND INSENSITIVE REMODELING.

 

AS A SIDE LIGHT, MR. KAISER ALSO HAS A COLLECTION OF 300 ANTIQUE WALKING STICKS; AND OVER 50 GERMAN AND AUSTRIAN IMPERIAL SWORDS. HE ALSO DOES “KITTY RESCUE”, WITH MANY CATS COMING AND GOING ALL THE TIME. THANK GOD FOR TILE FLOORS!

The Real Black Dahlia tour

Kim Cooper
0 1.1k
Share

“This bus tour… has established itself as an L.A. classic.” -The Los Angeles Times

The Black Dahlia murder in 1947 is the most compelling unsolved crime Los Angeles has ever known. What Jack the Ripper is to London, the Torso Killer to Cleveland, the Black Dahlia is to L.A. And yet unlike those other cases, the name Black Dahlia refers not to the killer, but to the victim. What was it about Elizabeth Short that keeps her the object of obsessive fascination by writers, musicians, artists, filmmakers, cops and readers, more than sixty years after she was slain?

The Real Black Dahlia Crime Bus Tour seeks to answer this question by intimately exploring the last weeks of Elizabeth Short’s life, asking not “who killed her?” but “who was she?”

The tour takes us from the human hustle of Main Street to the serene lobby of the Biltmore (the second-to-last place she was seen alive), to the newspaper offices and the Greyhound station where she checked her bags, and concludes at the site where her bisected body was found in Leimert Park and with a little known suspect who lived nearby.

From the few personal possessions she left behind to the friends who scarcely knew her, from the mass hysteria of the investigation with its fruitless leads, wacko suspects and false confessions, the tour reveals all that’s known about this enigmatic black-haired girl who reinvented herself at whim, and shows how she came to be the unfortunate symbol of her time and place.
